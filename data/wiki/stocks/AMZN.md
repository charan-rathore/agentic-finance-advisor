# Amazon.com, Inc. (AMZN)

## Summary

Amazon is a US-listed mega-cap conglomerate operating in the E-commerce and Cloud sectors. It spans e-commerce, cloud computing, advertising, and digital subscriptions. Rated as Growth/Cyclical, it is one of the heaviest weights in the S&P 500 and a constituent of the Nasdaq 100.

## Recent Price Action

AMZN is currently trading at $270.55, with a daily volume of 28,676,703 shares.

## News & Sentiment

Recent market news has focused on other major technology players, particularly Nvidia and Apple. For AMZN, market sentiment continues to revolve around its core strengths in e-commerce and cloud computing, with investors closely watching for operational efficiencies and growth trajectories in AWS.

## Business segments (per the most recent 10-K)

| Segment | What it does |
|---|---|
| North America | First-party retail, third-party seller services, Prime, advertising, Whole Foods |
| International | Same offerings outside the United States and Canada |
| Amazon Web Services (AWS) | Cloud infrastructure, platform, and software services |

AWS contributes a large share of consolidated operating income despite being
a smaller share of revenue. Advertising is a fast-growing, high-margin layer
inside the retail segments.

## Key financial signals to watch

* Total net sales growth, segmented by region
* AWS revenue growth and operating margin
* Advertising services revenue
* Free cash flow (capital intensity dropped meaningfully after the 2022-2023
  capex peak, then re-accelerated for AI infrastructure)
* Headcount and total employees

## Risk factors (from 10-K filings)

* Antitrust scrutiny in the United States and European Union
* Hyperscaler competition with Microsoft Azure and Google Cloud
* Generative AI competitive dynamics affecting AWS positioning
* Currency exposure on international revenue
* Capital intensity of AWS data centre buildouts

## Key Risks

Specific material risks for Amazon include ongoing [[E-commerce Maturation]] leading to margin pressure, intensified [[Cloud Infrastructure]] competition for AWS, rising [[Labor Cost Inflation]], potential for [[Big Tech Breakup]] due to antitrust scrutiny, and currency/regulatory challenges impacting [[International Expansion]].

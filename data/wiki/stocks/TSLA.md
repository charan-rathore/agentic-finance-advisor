# Tesla, Inc. (TSLA)

Tesla designs and sells electric vehicles, energy generation and storage products,
and is investing heavily in autonomy software (Full Self-Driving) and humanoid
robotics. It is a S&P 500 and Nasdaq 100 constituent.

## Business segments

| Segment | What it does |
|---|---|
| Automotive | Model 3, Model Y, Model S, Model X, Cybertruck, Semi |
| Energy generation and storage | Powerwall, Megapack, solar |
| Services and other | Vehicle service, insurance, used vehicle sales |

The automotive segment dominates revenue. Energy storage has been the fastest-
growing line item by percentage in recent quarters and is approaching a
meaningful share of operating income.

## Key financial signals to watch

* Vehicle deliveries by region (China, Europe, North America)
* Automotive gross margin excluding regulatory credits
* Energy storage deployments measured in gigawatt-hours
* Free cash flow and capex
* AI / robotics related investments and headcount
* Full Self-Driving subscription and one-time take rates

## Risk factors (from 10-K filings)

* EV demand cyclicality and price competition (notably in China)
* Regulatory risk around autonomy claims and software performance
* Reliance on ramp execution at the Berlin and Texas factories
* Concentration risk on key personnel
* Litigation related to autonomous driving incidents

## How retail investors usually access it

TSLA is a top weight in the S&P 500 and Nasdaq 100. Direct ownership requires a
US brokerage account. Indian investors can access TSLA through international
ETFs and funds offered by Indian AMCs, or via LRS-funded US brokerages.

# JPMorgan Chase & Co. (JPM)

JPMorgan Chase is the largest US bank by total assets and one of the four
Globally Systemically Important Banks (G-SIBs) headquartered in the United
States. It is a S&P 500 constituent and a heavyweight in financial-sector
indices.

## Business segments

| Segment | What it does |
|---|---|
| Consumer & Community Banking | Retail banking, credit cards, home and auto lending |
| Corporate & Investment Bank | Markets, advisory, treasury services |
| Asset & Wealth Management | Active asset management, private bank |
| Commercial Banking | Mid-market and corporate banking |

## Key financial signals to watch

* Net interest income and net interest margin (sensitive to Federal Reserve
  policy rate)
* Provision for credit losses (proxy for the credit cycle)
* Common Equity Tier 1 (CET1) capital ratio (regulatory floor and stress test
  buffer)
* Investment banking fees (advisory, equity capital markets, debt capital
  markets)
* Markets revenue (FICC and Equities)

## Risk factors (from 10-K filings)

* Interest rate risk (the bank manages duration on a USD 4 trillion balance
  sheet)
* Credit risk concentration in cards, autos, and commercial real estate
* Regulatory capital requirements (Basel III endgame proposals)
* Cybersecurity and operational risk
* Macro recession risk affecting loan losses

## How retail investors usually access it

JPM is a top weight in S&P 500 financial-sector ETFs and is held by every S&P
500 index fund. Direct ownership requires a US brokerage account. Indian
investors typically gain exposure through US-mirror index funds offered by
Indian AMCs.

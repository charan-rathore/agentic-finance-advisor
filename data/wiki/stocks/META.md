# Meta Platforms, Inc. (META)

Meta operates the world's largest portfolio of consumer social platforms,
including Facebook, Instagram, WhatsApp, and Messenger. It is a S&P 500 and
Nasdaq 100 constituent and one of the "Magnificent Seven" mega-caps.

## Business segments

| Segment | What it does |
|---|---|
| Family of Apps | Advertising on Facebook, Instagram, Messenger, WhatsApp |
| Reality Labs | AR/VR hardware (Quest), software, and metaverse platforms |

The Family of Apps segment generates effectively all of Meta's profit. Reality
Labs has consistently posted multi-billion dollar operating losses and is
positioned as a long-term option, not a near-term profit driver.

## Key financial signals to watch

* Daily Active People (DAP) across the family of apps
* Average revenue per user (ARPU), particularly in higher-monetised regions
  like North America and Europe
* Capital expenditure (heavy ramp for AI training and inference infrastructure)
* Reality Labs operating loss and revenue
* Buyback pace and dividend policy (Meta initiated its first dividend in 2024)

## Risk factors (from 10-K filings)

* Regulatory risk: data privacy, antitrust, content moderation requirements in
  EU (DMA, DSA), US, India, and other jurisdictions
* Apple App Tracking Transparency and platform-level changes affecting ad targeting
* Competitive pressure from TikTok, YouTube Shorts, and emerging platforms
* AI capex returns (large cash outlay before clear monetisation)
* Reality Labs sustained losses

## How retail investors usually access it

META sits inside every S&P 500 and Nasdaq 100 index fund. Direct ownership
requires a US brokerage account. Indian investors can access META through
international funds or LRS-funded US brokerages.

# Berkshire Hathaway Inc. (BRK.B)

Berkshire Hathaway is a US-listed conglomerate that wholly owns operating
businesses in insurance, freight rail, energy, manufacturing, and retail, plus
a public-equity portfolio. The Class B share (BRK.B) is the retail-friendly
stock; the Class A share (BRK.A) trades at very high per-share prices and
serves the long-term institutional base.

## What you actually own when you hold BRK.B

| Bucket | What it is |
|---|---|
| Insurance float | GEICO, Berkshire Hathaway Reinsurance, BHPC. Generates a large pool of premiums Berkshire invests until claims are paid |
| Wholly owned operating businesses | BNSF Railway, Berkshire Hathaway Energy, Precision Castparts, See's Candies, Duracell, Dairy Queen, Pilot, etc. |
| Equity portfolio | Public US stocks held by the parent (top weights historically include AAPL, AXP, KO, MCO, OXY) |
| Cash and Treasuries | A large balance kept dry for opportunistic deployment |

## Key financial signals to watch

* Operating earnings (the figure Buffett asks readers to prioritise over GAAP
  net income)
* Insurance combined ratio and underwriting profit
* BNSF and BHE earnings trajectory
* Cash and short-term investments balance
* Buyback activity (active when the stock trades meaningfully below
  Berkshire's intrinsic-value estimate)

## Risk factors (from 10-K filings)

* Catastrophe insurance loss exposure
* Sensitivity to long-term US economic activity (rail, energy, retail)
* Concentration risk in the public equity portfolio (historically a large
  weight on a small number of names)
* Succession execution after Warren Buffett

## How retail investors usually access it

BRK.B is a S&P 500 constituent. It is held by every S&P 500 index fund. Direct
ownership of BRK.B requires a US brokerage account. Indian investors can access
BRK.B through international funds (e.g. Mirae Asset NYSE FANG+ ETF FoF does
not hold it; but several "S&P 500" mirror funds in India do) or LRS-funded US
accounts.

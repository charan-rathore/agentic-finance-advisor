# Reliance Industries Limited (RELIANCE.NS)

Reliance Industries Limited is India's largest private-sector company by market
capitalisation and revenue. It is the heaviest single weight in both the Nifty 50
and the BSE Sensex.

## Business segments

| Segment | What it does | Approx revenue contribution |
|---|---|---|
| Oil to Chemicals (O2C) | Refining, petrochemicals, fuel retail | About 55 percent |
| Reliance Jio (Digital Services) | Telecom, broadband, enterprise services | About 15 percent |
| Reliance Retail | Grocery, fashion, consumer electronics, JioMart | About 25 percent |
| Oil and Gas (E&P) | Domestic gas exploration | Low single digits |
| New Energy | Solar, hydrogen, batteries (ramp-up phase) | Pre-revenue |

Source: Reliance Industries FY24 annual report. Mix shifts year on year.

## Key financial signals to watch

* Consolidated revenue and EBITDA quarterly trend
* O2C gross refining margins, which depend on crude price spreads
* Jio ARPU (average revenue per user) and subscriber adds
* Reliance Retail store count, footfalls, and same-store sales growth
* Net debt and net debt to EBITDA ratio
* Capital expenditure on the New Energy programme

## Risk factors (from filings)

* Crude oil price volatility for the O2C business
* Telecom tariff competition affecting Jio ARPU
* Execution risk on the New Energy capital expenditure programme
* Regulatory risk in retail and telecom (TRAI, MeitY)
* Currency risk on dollar denominated debt

## How retail investors usually access it

Most diversified equity mutual funds and all Nifty 50 index funds hold Reliance.
You typically do not need a direct position to gain exposure. A direct stock
position requires a Demat account and is suitable only for investors comfortable
with single-stock concentration risk.

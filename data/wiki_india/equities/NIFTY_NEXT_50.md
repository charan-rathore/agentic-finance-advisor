# Nifty Next 50 Index

The Nifty Next 50 represents 50 well-established companies that sit just below the
Nifty 50 in market capitalisation. It is often called the "tomorrow's Nifty 50"
because constituents frequently graduate into the headline index after sustained
growth.

## What it tracks

* 50 large-cap Indian companies ranked 51 through 100 by free-float market cap
* Covers high-growth sectors like consumer discretionary, capital goods, and pharma
  more heavily than the Nifty 50
* Reviewed semi-annually alongside the Nifty 50

## How it differs from Nifty 50

| Feature | Nifty 50 | Nifty Next 50 |
|---|---|---|
| Constituents | Top 50 by free-float | Ranks 51 to 100 |
| Volatility | Lower | Higher |
| Historical CAGR (long run, indicative) | 12 to 14 percent | 13 to 16 percent |
| Sector tilt | Financials, IT, Energy | Consumer, Capital Goods, Pharma |
| Suitability | Core holding for beginners | Satellite holding for medium-risk investors |

## How to invest

* **Index funds** such as ICICI Prudential Nifty Next 50 Index Fund or DSP Nifty Next 50 Index Fund
* **ETFs** such as Nippon India ETF Junior BeES

Expense ratios for direct-plan index funds tracking this index are typically 0.20
to 0.40 percent.

## Why a beginner might add it

A common starter portfolio is 70 percent Nifty 50 plus 30 percent Nifty Next 50
to capture the next tier of large-cap growth without venturing into mid-cap
volatility. This is sometimes called the "Nifty 100 split".

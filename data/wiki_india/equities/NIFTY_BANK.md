# Nifty Bank Index

Nifty Bank tracks the 12 most liquid and large Indian banking stocks listed on the
National Stock Exchange. It is the most heavily traded sectoral index in India and
is widely used as a hedging proxy for financial-services exposure.

## Constituents (representative)

HDFC Bank, ICICI Bank, State Bank of India, Axis Bank, Kotak Mahindra Bank,
Federal Bank, Bank of Baroda, IndusInd Bank, IDFC First Bank, Punjab National Bank,
AU Small Finance Bank, Bandhan Bank.

Weights are reviewed semi-annually. HDFC Bank and ICICI Bank typically dominate the
index with roughly 50 percent combined weight.

## What drives it

* RBI repo rate decisions (cost of funds for banks)
* Credit growth (loan book expansion across the system)
* Asset quality cycle (gross and net NPAs)
* Net Interest Margins, which compress when deposit costs rise faster than lending rates
* Regulatory capital and provisioning norms (Basel III, RBI prudential framework)

## How to invest

* **Sectoral index funds and ETFs** such as Nippon India ETF Nifty Bank BeES or ICICI Prudential Nifty Bank ETF
* **Sectoral mutual funds** such as ICICI Prudential Banking and Financial Services Fund (active)

Sectoral exposure should usually not exceed 10 to 15 percent of an equity portfolio
for a beginner. The Nifty 50 already has 33 percent in financial services.

## Risk profile

Nifty Bank is more volatile than the Nifty 50 because it concentrates one sector.
Drawdowns of 20 to 30 percent in a single year have happened multiple times since 2008.
This is not a "core" holding for a first-time investor.

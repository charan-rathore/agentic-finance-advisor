# Nifty 50 Index

The Nifty 50 is India's flagship benchmark, tracking the 50 largest, most liquid
companies listed on the National Stock Exchange (NSE). It is computed and maintained
by NSE Indices Limited and is the primary barometer of large-cap Indian equities.

## What it tracks

* 50 of the largest companies by free-float market capitalisation across 13 sectors
* Constituents are reviewed every six months (semi-annual rebalance, typically March and September)
* Free-float methodology, meaning only shares available for public trading are counted

## Sector composition (approximate)

| Sector | Approx weight |
|---|---|
| Financial Services | 33% |
| Information Technology | 13% |
| Oil & Gas | 12% |
| Consumer Goods | 9% |
| Automobile | 7% |
| Metals, Pharma, Telecom and Others | 26% |

## Historical returns (long-run, indicative)

The Nifty 50 has delivered roughly 12 to 14 percent CAGR over rolling 20-year windows
(NSE total return index, 2004 to 2024). Short windows of 1 to 3 years can be flat or
negative. Patience and a 5 plus year horizon are how SIP investors realise the
historical premium.

## How to invest in it

You cannot buy the index itself. You can buy products that track it:

* **Index Mutual Funds** such as UTI Nifty 50 Index Fund or HDFC Index Nifty 50
* **ETFs** such as Nippon India ETF Nifty BeES or ICICI Prudential Nifty 50 ETF
* **Index futures and options** (advanced, not for beginners)

Expense ratios of direct-plan index funds tracking Nifty 50 are typically between
0.10 and 0.30 percent per year, far lower than active funds (1 to 2 percent).

## Why it matters for retail investors

The Nifty 50 is the simplest way for a first-time investor to get diversified
equity exposure to India. It captures the country's largest businesses across
sectors and removes single-stock risk.

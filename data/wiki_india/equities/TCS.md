# Tata Consultancy Services (TCS.NS)

Tata Consultancy Services is India's largest IT services company by revenue and
market capitalisation, and a flagship of the Tata Group. It is a top-five weight
in the Nifty 50 and a global Tier-1 IT services provider alongside Accenture,
Infosys, Wipro, and HCLTech.

## Business mix (representative)

| Segment | What it does | Approx revenue share |
|---|---|---|
| BFSI | Banking, financial services, insurance technology | About 35 percent |
| Consumer Business | Retail, travel, hospitality | About 15 percent |
| Communications, Media, Information Services | Telecom, media platforms | About 18 percent |
| Manufacturing and Energy | Industry vertical solutions | About 12 percent |
| Life Sciences and Healthcare | Pharma, medical devices, healthcare IT | About 10 percent |
| Public Services and Others | Government and public sector | About 10 percent |

Source: TCS FY24 annual report.

## Key financial signals to watch

* USD revenue growth in constant currency (the headline industry KPI)
* Operating margin (TCS historically runs 24 to 26 percent operating margin)
* Total Contract Value (TCV) of new deal wins, reported each quarter
* Attrition rate (an industry stress signal)
* Headcount and utilisation
* Free cash flow conversion (TCS targets greater than 100 percent of net income)

## Risk factors (from filings)

* Demand slowdown in the United States and Europe (about 80 percent of revenue)
* Wage inflation, both onshore and offshore
* Visa policy and immigration risk for North American operations
* Currency risk, since revenue is in USD, GBP, and EUR while costs are partly in INR
* Generative AI disruption on commodity application services and BPO

## How retail investors usually access it

TCS is held by every Nifty 50 index fund and most diversified large-cap funds.
Direct stock exposure is straightforward through any Demat account but, like
RELIANCE, exposes you to single-stock concentration risk.

# Nippon India Multi Asset Fund (Direct Growth)

A multi-asset hybrid fund managed by Nippon Life India Asset Management. SEBI
defines multi-asset as a fund that invests in at least three asset classes with
a minimum 10 percent in each.

## Why multi-asset funds exist

A multi-asset fund delivers diversification across uncorrelated asset classes in
a single product. The intuition is that gold tends to do well when equities
struggle, and international equity often diversifies India-specific risk. The
fund manager rebalances across asset classes based on the scheme mandate.

## Tax treatment (post Budget 2024 framework)

If the fund holds 65 percent or more in Indian equities, it is taxed as an
equity fund. Otherwise it is taxed as a debt or non-equity fund. Investors
should check the latest factsheet for the current category tag, which determines
LTCG rate (12.5 percent above INR 1.25 lakh, equity) versus slab rate (debt/other).

## Where it fits

* Conservative investors who want a single product and built-in rebalancing
* Investors who do not want to manage gold, international equity, and debt
  positions separately
* Not optimal for tax efficiency if the fund tips below 65 percent equity

## Cost

Direct plan expense ratio is typically 0.40 to 0.80 percent.

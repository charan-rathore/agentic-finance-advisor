# Quant Active Fund (Direct Growth)

A multi-cap fund managed by Quant Mutual Fund. SEBI's multi-cap definition
requires at least 25 percent allocation each to large, mid, and small caps,
making this category structurally more aggressive than a flexi-cap.

## How multi-cap differs from flexi-cap

| Feature | Multi Cap | Flexi Cap |
|---|---|---|
| Minimum large-cap | 25 percent | No minimum |
| Minimum mid-cap | 25 percent | No minimum |
| Minimum small-cap | 25 percent | No minimum |
| Manager flexibility | Limited by floors | High |
| Volatility | Higher (forced small/mid floors) | Lower (manager can avoid small caps) |

The multi-cap structural floor of 25 percent each in mid and small caps means
this category typically delivers higher returns and higher drawdowns than
flexi-cap funds in the same period.

## Quant's style

Quant runs a momentum-tilted, high-turnover process. The manager rotates
sector positions actively. This style works well in trending markets and can
underperform in choppy, mean-reverting markets.

## Cost

Direct plan expense ratio is typically 0.55 to 0.80 percent.

## Position sizing

Best used as a satellite holding (10 to 20 percent of equity) for investors with
a 7 plus year horizon. Not a "single-fund" choice for a beginner because of the
mandatory mid and small cap floors.

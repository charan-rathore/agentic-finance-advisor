# Axis ELSS Tax Saver Fund (Direct Growth)

An Equity Linked Savings Scheme (ELSS). ELSS funds qualify for income tax
deduction under Section 80C of the Income Tax Act, up to INR 1.5 lakh per
financial year, in the old tax regime.

## How the lock-in works

Each SIP instalment is locked for 3 years from the date that instalment is
allotted units. A SIP started in April 2026 will fully unlock only by April 2029,
because each month's units have a separate 3-year clock.

## Tax benefit (old regime only)

* Up to INR 1,50,000 of investment in 80C instruments per year is deductible
* At a 30 percent marginal rate, that is up to INR 46,800 of tax saved per year
  (including 4 percent cess)
* The new tax regime (default since FY 2024) does not allow this deduction

## When ELSS makes sense

* You file under the old tax regime and have not yet exhausted 80C with EPF, PPF,
  home loan principal, life insurance, or NPS Tier 1
* Your horizon is 5 plus years (the 3-year lock-in is the minimum, but the equity
  asset class needs longer to deliver)
* You want equity exposure in a tax-efficient wrapper

## Cost

Direct plan expense ratio is typically 0.80 to 1.00 percent. Regular plan is
1.50 to 1.80 percent. Across a 10 plus year hold, the direct plan saving is
material.

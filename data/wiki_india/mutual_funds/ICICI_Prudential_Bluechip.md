# ICICI Prudential Bluechip Fund (Direct Growth)

An actively managed large-cap fund. SEBI defines a large-cap fund as one that
invests at least 80 percent of its corpus in the top 100 companies by market
capitalisation.

## Mandate

The fund invests primarily in well-established, large companies (top 100 by market
cap) and selectively adds 0 to 20 percent in mid-caps or other equity instruments.
The portfolio typically holds 60 to 70 stocks across financials, IT, energy,
consumer goods, and pharmaceuticals.

## Active vs index trade-off

Active large-cap funds in India have historically struggled to outperform the
Nifty 100 on a long-term, post-fee basis. SEBI's own SPIVA-style data shows
roughly 70 to 80 percent of large-cap active funds trail their index over rolling
10-year windows. The case for an active fund here rests on lower drawdowns, not
higher long-run returns.

## Cost

Direct plan expense ratio is typically 1.00 to 1.20 percent. Regular plan is
typically 1.60 to 1.80 percent. Compare against an index fund at 0.20 percent
when deciding.

## Where it fits

* Investors who explicitly want active management for downside protection
* Investors who already have an index fund and want a complementary diversified
  large-cap sleeve

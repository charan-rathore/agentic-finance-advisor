# Parag Parikh Flexi Cap Fund (Direct Growth)

A flexi-cap equity fund managed by PPFAS Mutual Fund. The scheme is widely cited
as a gateway product for first-time long-term equity investors because of its
disciplined, value-tilted, multi-cap mandate and substantial overseas equity sleeve.

## Mandate

A flexi-cap fund can invest at least 65 percent in Indian equities across large,
mid, and small caps. The PPFAS scheme additionally allocates up to 35 percent to
overseas equities (subject to SEBI overseas investment limits, which RBI revises
periodically). The portfolio typically holds 25 to 30 stocks.

## Why it shows up in beginner recommendations

* Single-fund equity exposure across market caps and geographies
* High portfolio turnover discipline. Concentrated, conviction-driven holdings
* Long-term investor base, which keeps cash flow stable for the fund manager

## Key risks

* Overseas equity sleeve exposes you to global drawdowns and currency risk
* SEBI or RBI tightening of overseas investment limits can cap inflows
* Concentration risk because the portfolio holds fewer names than peers

## Costs

Direct plan expense ratio is typically 0.60 to 0.80 percent (verify on the
factsheet). Regular plan expense ratio is roughly 1.30 to 1.50 percent. The
direct plan saves more than 0.50 percent per year over a long horizon.

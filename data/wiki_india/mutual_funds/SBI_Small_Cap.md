# SBI Small Cap Fund (Direct Growth)

A small-cap equity fund managed by SBI Mutual Fund. SEBI defines small caps as
companies ranked 251 and below by market capitalisation.

## What small-cap funds are and are not

Small-cap funds invest at least 65 percent in companies ranked 251 and lower by
market capitalisation. These are companies that are typically less liquid, less
researched, and more volatile than large-caps.

* Higher long-run upside, historically
* Drawdowns of 50 to 60 percent in bad years are not unusual (FY09, FY16, FY20, FY23)
* Liquidity risk in the underlying portfolio. Some funds soft-close inflows when
  AUM grows beyond what the small-cap universe can absorb

## Position sizing

For most retail investors, small-cap allocation should not exceed 10 to 20 percent
of the equity portion of the portfolio. Beyond that, the volatility starts driving
behavioural mistakes (panic selling at lows).

## Cost

Direct plan expense ratio is typically 0.70 to 0.90 percent. Regular plan is
typically 1.60 to 1.80 percent. Active management is more defensible in small
caps than in large caps because index coverage and analyst coverage are weaker.

## When this fund makes sense

* Existing equity investor with a 7 plus year horizon and high risk tolerance
* As a 10 to 15 percent satellite holding alongside a Nifty 50 or flexi-cap core
* Not appropriate as a first SIP for a new investor

# Investment Taxation in India

Understanding how your investment gains are taxed is essential before choosing
any financial product. India taxes investments differently based on:

1. **What you invested in** (equity vs. debt vs. gold)
2. **How long you held it** (Short-Term vs. Long-Term)
3. **Which tax regime you chose** (Old vs. New)

---

## The Two Tax Regimes (FY 2025–26)

| Feature | Old Regime | New Regime (Default since FY 2024) |
|---------|-----------|-----------------------------------|
| Standard Deduction | ₹50,000 | ₹75,000 |
| 80C (ELSS, PPF, etc.) | Up to ₹1.5L | Not available |
| HRA exemption | Yes | No |
| Tax slabs | More slabs | Simpler, lower base rates |
| Who benefits | High deductions (>₹3.75L) | Fewer/no deductions |

**Recommendation for most investors with a simple salary + SIP setup:**
The new regime is simpler and often better. If you have a home loan and maximise
80C + 80D + HRA, the old regime can still win — run the numbers for your case.

---

## Capital Gains Tax on Equity (Stocks + Equity Mutual Funds)

Equity means any fund with 65%+ allocation to Indian equities.

| Holding Period | Tax Category | Rate |
|----------------|-------------|------|
| < 12 months | Short-Term Capital Gain (STCG) | **20%** flat (Budget 2024) |
| ≥ 12 months | Long-Term Capital Gain (LTCG) | **12.5%** above ₹1.25 lakh/year |

### LTCG Exemption: ₹1.25 lakh per year
You can book up to ₹1.25 lakh of long-term equity gains every financial year
completely tax-free. This is a valuable annual window for "tax harvesting":
selling profitable units and immediately buying them back to reset your cost basis.

**Example:**
- Invested ₹5L in a Nifty 50 fund in Jan 2024.
- Sold in Jan 2026 for ₹6.5L. Gain = ₹1.5L.
- Taxable gain = ₹1.5L − ₹1.25L exemption = ₹25,000.
- Tax = 12.5% × ₹25,000 = **₹3,125**. (Not ₹18,750 on the full gain.)

### Equity LTCG: Grandfathering
Gains on equity bought before 31 Jan 2018 are grandfathered at the NAV on that
date. Practically irrelevant for most new investors.

## Capital Gains Tax on Debt Mutual Funds

**Changed by Finance Act 2023 (effective 1 Apr 2023):**
All debt fund gains (regardless of holding period) are now taxed at your
**income tax slab rate** — no LTCG benefit, no indexation.

| Holding Period | Rate |
|----------------|------|
| Any | Income slab rate (5%, 10%, 15%, 20%, 30%) |

This made debt funds less attractive vs. Fixed Deposits (which are also taxed
at slab rates) — the only remaining advantage is liquidity and no TDS on gains
under ₹5,000 per payout event.

**Exception:** Funds with 35–65% equity are taxed as debt under the new rules.

## Capital Gains Tax on Gold

| Instrument | Holding | Tax |
|-----------|---------|-----|
| Physical Gold | < 24 months | Slab rate |
| Physical Gold | ≥ 24 months | 12.5% LTCG (Budget 2024, no indexation) |
| Sovereign Gold Bond (SGB) | Held to maturity (8 years) | **Tax-free** |
| Gold ETF / Gold MF | < 24 months | Slab rate |
| Gold ETF / Gold MF | ≥ 24 months | 12.5% LTCG |

**SGBs are the most tax-efficient gold investment for a long-term horizon.**

## Section 80C: Tax-Saving Investments (Old Regime Only)

Under the old regime, investments up to **₹1,50,000** per year under Section 80C
reduce your taxable income. Popular 80C instruments:

| Instrument | Lock-in | Risk | Return (approx) |
|-----------|---------|------|----------------|
| ELSS Mutual Fund | 3 years | Market risk | 10–14% historical |
| PPF | 15 years (partial after 7) | Zero | 7.1% (govt-set) |
| NSC | 5 years | Zero | 7.7% |
| Tax-saving FD | 5 years | Zero | 6–7% |
| ULIP | 5 years | Market risk | Varies (high charges) |
| EPF (employee) | Retirement | Zero | 8.1% (FY 25) |

**ELSS is the recommended 80C instrument for investors with a 5+ year horizon:**
shortest lock-in (3 years) + equity upside + tax saving.

---

## Section 80D: Health Insurance Premium

| Who Insured | Deduction Limit |
|------------|----------------|
| Self + spouse + children | ₹25,000 |
| Parents (< 60 years) | + ₹25,000 |
| Parents (60+ years) | + ₹50,000 |

Total possible deduction: up to **₹75,000** per year.

---

## Dividend Taxation

As of FY 2021–22, dividends from stocks and mutual funds are taxed at your
**income slab rate** (not a flat 10% as before). TDS is deducted at 10% if
dividends exceed ₹5,000 in a financial year from a single company/fund.

**Implication:** Growth option funds are tax-superior to IDCW (dividend) option
for most investors in the 20–30% tax bracket. Prefer Growth option.

---

## TDS on Mutual Fund Redemptions

| Scenario | TDS Rate |
|----------|---------|
| Equity fund, NRI investor | 15% (STCG), 10% (LTCG) |
| Equity fund, resident Indian | **No TDS** |
| Debt fund, NRI investor | 30% (slab) |
| Debt fund, resident Indian | No TDS |

Resident Indians self-report capital gains in ITR-2 — no TDS is deducted by AMCs.

## Quick Decision Guide

**Should I invest in ELSS or PPF?**
- Need liquidity in < 5 years → ELSS (3-year lock-in)
- Want zero risk + guaranteed return → PPF
- In the 30% bracket + long horizon → ELSS wins on post-tax returns historically

**Debt fund vs. FD?**
- Short-term (< 3 years), high tax bracket → FD may be simpler (same tax, DICGC insurance)
- Long-term, need flexibility, low bracket → Debt fund for better liquidity

**SGB vs. Gold ETF?**
- Holding 8 years → SGB (tax-free maturity + 2.5% interest)
- Need liquidity anytime → Gold ETF

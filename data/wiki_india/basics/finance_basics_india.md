# Indian Personal Finance Basics

This page is the **foundation** of the India knowledge base. The AI advisor
reads it when answering beginner questions. It is intentionally written in
plain English so any first-time investor can follow it.

---

## What Is Investing and Why Does It Matter?

Keeping money in a savings bank account earns 2.5–4% interest per year.
Inflation in India typically runs at 4–6%. That means your purchasing power
shrinks every year you don't invest.

**Investing** means putting money to work so it grows faster than inflation.
The most common options for Indian retail investors are:

| Option | Who It's For | Risk | Typical Return |
|--------|-------------|------|----------------|
| Savings/FD | Everyone | Very low | 5–7% |
| PPF (Public Provident Fund) | Long-term, tax-free | Very low | 7.1% (govt-set) |
| Mutual Funds (Debt) | 1–5 year horizon | Low–medium | 6–9% |
| Mutual Funds (Equity) | 5+ year horizon | Medium–high | 10–14% historically |
| Direct Stocks (NSE/BSE) | Experienced investors | High | Varies widely |
| Gold (SGBs/ETFs) | Hedge, 5+ years | Medium | ~8–10% historical |

## The SIP Habit: Small Amounts, Big Impact

**SIP = Systematic Investment Plan.** You invest a fixed amount (as low as ₹500/month)
into a mutual fund automatically, every month.

Why SIP works:
1. **Rupee-cost averaging** — you buy more units when NAV is low, fewer when high.
2. **Compounding** — returns earn returns. ₹5,000/month at 12% for 20 years = ₹49.9 lakh.
3. **No need to time the market** — behavioural research shows most retail investors lose
   money trying to time entries and exits.

**Key number to remember:** At 12% annual return, money doubles every ~6 years
(Rule of 72: 72 ÷ 12 = 6).

## Types of Mutual Funds: A Plain-Language Guide

### Equity Funds (Stocks)
Invest primarily in company stocks listed on NSE/BSE. Suitable for 5+ year goals.

- **Large-cap**: Top 100 companies by market cap (RELIANCE, TCS, HDFC Bank).
  Lower risk than mid/small cap. Example: Mirae Asset Large Cap Fund.
- **Flexi-cap**: Fund manager chooses mix of large, mid, small. Good starting point.
  Example: Parag Parikh Flexi Cap Fund.
- **ELSS (Tax Saver)**: Equity fund with 3-year lock-in. Qualifies for ₹1.5 lakh
  deduction under Section 80C. Example: Mirae Asset ELSS Tax Saver Fund.
- **Index Fund**: Passively copies an index like Nifty 50. Lowest cost (expense ratio
  ~0.1–0.2%). Recommended for beginners. Example: UTI Nifty 50 Index Fund.

### Debt Funds (Bonds/Fixed Income)
Invest in government/corporate bonds. Lower risk than equity.

- **Liquid Fund**: Invests in instruments maturing in < 91 days. Best for emergency fund
  or cash parking. Example: SBI Liquid Fund.
- **Short Duration Fund**: 1–3 year maturity bonds. Better return than FD with similar
  safety (for investment-grade funds). Example: HDFC Short Term Debt Fund.

### Hybrid Funds
Mix of equity + debt, automatically rebalanced.

- **Balanced Advantage Fund**: Reduces equity exposure when markets are expensive, increases
  it when cheap. Good for conservative equity investors. Example: ICICI Balanced Advantage Fund.

### Key Terms
| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| NAV | Net Asset Value — the per-unit price of a fund |
| AUM | Assets Under Management — total money managed by the fund |
| Expense Ratio | Annual fee charged by the fund (lower is better) |
| Direct Plan | Buy from AMC directly, no distributor commission (cheaper) |
| Regular Plan | Buy through a broker/distributor (costlier, same portfolio) |
| Exit Load | Penalty if you redeem before the lock-in/cooling period |
| CAGR | Compound Annual Growth Rate — annualised return |

## Key Indian Financial Regulators

| Regulator | Role |
|-----------|------|
| **SEBI** (Securities and Exchange Board of India) | Regulates stock exchanges (NSE, BSE), mutual funds, brokers |
| **RBI** (Reserve Bank of India) | Sets monetary policy, repo rate; regulates banks |
| **AMFI** (Association of Mutual Funds in India) | Self-regulatory body for AMCs; mandates NAV publication |
| **IRDAI** | Regulates insurance |
| **PFRDA** | Regulates NPS (National Pension System) |

## The Indian Stock Exchanges

- **NSE (National Stock Exchange)**: Largest by trading volume. Main index: **Nifty 50**
  (top 50 companies). Symbols end in `.NS` (e.g. TCS.NS).
- **BSE (Bombay Stock Exchange)**: Oldest exchange (1875). Main index: **Sensex** (30 companies).
  Symbols end in `.BO`.

Most retail investors trade on NSE. Both exchanges list the same large companies.

## Understanding the Nifty 50

The Nifty 50 is India's benchmark index — it tracks the 50 largest, most liquid companies
listed on NSE. Sector breakdown (approximate):

| Sector | Weight |
|--------|--------|
| Financial Services | ~33% |
| IT | ~13% |
| Oil & Gas | ~12% |
| Consumer Goods | ~9% |
| Automobile | ~7% |
| Others | ~26% |

**Historical performance:** Nifty 50 has delivered ~12–14% CAGR over 20+ years.
Short-term (1–3 year) returns can be negative. Patience is the edge.

## How to Start: A 4-Step Beginner Plan

1. **Build 3–6 months of expenses as emergency fund** in a liquid mutual fund or FD.
2. **Get insurance first**: term life (if dependents) and health insurance.
3. **Open a free direct mutual fund account**: Zerodha Coin, Groww, Kuvera, MF Central (AMFI).
4. **Start 1–2 SIPs**:
   - Nifty 50 Index Fund (for long-term wealth)
   - ELSS fund (for Section 80C tax benefit)

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Chasing past returns**: Last year's best performer is rarely next year's best.
- **Stopping SIPs during a market crash**: This is exactly when you should continue —
  you're buying more units at a lower price.
- **Ignoring expense ratios**: A 1% difference in annual fees compounds dramatically.
  Prefer Direct plans over Regular plans.
- **Over-diversifying**: 2–3 funds covering equity + debt is enough. 10 funds don't
  reduce risk — they just replicate the market at higher cost.
- **No clear goal**: Tie every investment to a goal (retirement in 20 years, house in 5).
  The goal determines the asset class.

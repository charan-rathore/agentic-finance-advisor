A **Systematic Investment Plan (SIP)** is a standing instruction you give a
mutual fund to debit a fixed amount from your bank account every month and use
it to buy units of the fund at the prevailing NAV.

**Why SIP works:**

1. **Rupee-cost averaging.** When the NAV is low, your fixed rupee amount buys
   more units; when it is high, it buys fewer. The average price you pay smooths
   out over time.
2. **Compounding.** Returns earn returns. INR 5,000 invested every month at a
   12 percent annual return compounds to roughly INR 11.6 lakh in 10 years and
   INR 49.9 lakh in 20 years.
3. **Behaviour defence.** A standing instruction removes the daily decision
   of whether to invest, which is where most retail investors lose to their
   own emotions.

**How long?** Equity SIPs need 5 plus years to express their statistical edge.
ELSS SIPs are locked in for 3 years per instalment by SEBI rules.

**Where to start?** Begin with one Direct plan index fund or flexi-cap fund. Two
funds is plenty. AMFI-registered platforms include MF Central, Coin, Groww,
Kuvera, and the AMC websites themselves.

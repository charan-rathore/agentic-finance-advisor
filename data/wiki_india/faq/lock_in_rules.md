Lock-in is the minimum holding period imposed by the product (and sometimes by
SEBI rules). You cannot redeem before this period ends except through specific
permitted routes.

| Product | Lock-in | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **ELSS** | **3 years per SIP instalment** | Each SIP unit has its own clock. Started in April 2026, the last instalment unlocks April 2029. SEBI rule, no early exit |
| **PPF** | **15 years** at maturity | Partial withdrawals allowed after year 7. Loan against PPF available after year 3. Five-year extensions possible |
| **NPS Tier 1** | **Until age 60** | At 60, 60 percent of corpus is tax-free, 40 percent must purchase an annuity (taxed as income). Limited early exit for serious illness or premature emergence |
| **Tax-saving FD (5-year)** | **5 years** | Premature withdrawal generally not allowed |
| **NSC** | **5 years** | Locked until maturity |
| **ULIP** | **5 years** | Premature withdrawal forfeits tax benefit |
| **Sukanya Samriddhi (SSY)** | **21 years** or daughter's marriage after age 18 | Specifically for a girl child below age 10 |
| **Sovereign Gold Bond (SGB)** | **8 years** maturity | Early redemption window every 6 months from year 5 |
| **Open-ended equity MF** | **None**, but exit load up to 1 percent if redeemed within 365 days | Not a regulatory lock-in |
| **Index fund / ETF** | **None** | Trade or redeem any business day |

**A common pitfall:** Investors think ELSS unlocks 3 years after they started
the SIP. It doesn't. Each monthly instalment has a 3-year clock. A 5-year SIP
in ELSS has its last instalment locked until 8 years after the SIP started.

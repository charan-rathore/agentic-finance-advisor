For Indian large-cap exposure, **index funds tend to win on a long-term,
post-fee basis.** SEBI's SPIVA-style data has consistently shown that 70 to
80 percent of active large-cap funds underperform the Nifty 100 over rolling
10-year windows.

**Why index funds work:**

* Expense ratio of 0.10 to 0.30 percent vs 1.00 to 1.20 percent for active
  large-cap. Over 20 years, the fee gap alone compounds to a meaningful
  fraction of the corpus
* No manager risk. The fund cannot deviate from the benchmark
* Tax efficiency. Lower portfolio churn means fewer triggered capital gains
  inside the fund

**When active makes sense:**

* **Mid-cap and small-cap.** Index coverage and analyst coverage thin out
  rapidly below the top 100 by market cap. Active managers with strong
  research teams can add value here
* **Flexi-cap.** A capable manager can shift across cap buckets with the cycle
* **ELSS.** Most ELSS funds are active. Pick one with a 10 plus year track
  record and a stable manager

**A defensible default for a first-time investor:** Nifty 50 or Nifty 500
index fund as the core (60 to 80 percent of equity), one flexi-cap or ELSS
active fund as a satellite (20 to 40 percent). Stop there.

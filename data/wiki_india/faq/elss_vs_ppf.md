Both qualify for **Section 80C** (under the old tax regime, up to INR 1.5 lakh
per year). They serve different purposes.

| Attribute | ELSS | PPF |
|---|---|---|
| Asset class | Equity | Sovereign |
| Lock-in | 3 years per SIP instalment | 15 years (loan/partial after 7) |
| Indicative return | 10 to 14 percent CAGR (historical) | 7.1 percent (Q1 FY26, set quarterly) |
| Risk | Equity (high) | Zero |
| Maturity tax | LTCG 12.5 percent above INR 1.25 lakh per FY | Tax-free (EEE) |

**Quick guide:**

* Horizon 5 plus years and you can stomach equity volatility, **ELSS** has
  delivered the highest historical post-tax return in the 80C bucket
* Want zero risk and a 15-year retirement or child-education sleeve, **PPF**
  is unmatched
* For most working-age investors, a sensible split is **both**: PPF for the
  safe sleeve, ELSS for the equity sleeve

**Important:** This deduction only applies under the old tax regime. The new
regime (default since FY 2024) does not allow 80C. Compare both regimes before
investing primarily for tax savings.

An emergency fund is the cash reserve that lets you survive a financial shock
(job loss, medical emergency, major repair) without breaking long-term
investments or running up high-cost credit card debt.

**Target:** **3 to 6 months of essential monthly expenses.** Add 1 to 2 months
if you are self-employed, a sole earner, or in a volatile industry.

"Essential" means rent or EMI, food, utilities, insurance premiums, school
fees, and minimum medication costs. Not Netflix and not weekend dinners.

**Where to keep it:**

| Vehicle | Liquidity | Indicative return |
|---|---|---|
| Savings bank account | Instant | 2.5 to 4 percent |
| Sweep-in FD | Instant on withdrawal | 4 to 6 percent |
| Liquid mutual fund | T plus 1 (instant up to INR 50,000) | 6 to 7 percent |

A practical mix: 1 month in your savings account, the rest in a liquid mutual
fund at the same AMC where you hold your equity SIPs.

**Important:** Build the emergency fund **before** starting an equity SIP. If
you start equity first, a job loss during a market drawdown forces you to sell
at the worst possible price.

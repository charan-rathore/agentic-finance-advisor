The most useful framework is the **50-30-20 rule** adapted for Indian salaried
investors:

* **50 percent of take-home pay** for needs (rent, EMIs, utilities, food,
  insurance, school fees)
* **30 percent for wants** (eating out, travel, subscriptions, lifestyle)
* **20 percent for savings and investments**

The 20 percent itself splits roughly:

| Bucket | Share |
|---|---|
| Emergency fund (until 3 to 6 months of expenses) | 5 to 10 percent of take-home |
| Equity SIP (long-term wealth) | 10 to 12 percent |
| Tax-saver or retirement (ELSS or NPS) | 3 to 5 percent |

**Worked example for INR 50,000 take-home:**

* Emergency fund SIP: INR 3,000 (until fully funded, then redirect to equity)
* Index fund SIP: INR 5,000
* ELSS SIP: INR 2,000
* Total: INR 10,000 per month, exactly 20 percent

**Step-up your SIP every year.** A 10 percent annual step-up matches typical
salary increments and meaningfully improves long-run outcomes without feeling
painful in the moment.

**The most important number is the one you can sustain.** A consistent INR
2,000 monthly for 20 years beats an enthusiastic INR 10,000 for 6 months.

*Educational only. Adapt to your fixed costs, dependents, and goals.*

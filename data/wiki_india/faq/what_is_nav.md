**NAV (Net Asset Value)** is the per-unit price of a mutual fund, calculated
once per business day after market close.

**Formula:**

NAV = (Total market value of the fund's portfolio + cash on hand - liabilities and
expenses) / Number of outstanding units

For example, if a fund holds INR 1,000 crore of stocks and bonds, has INR 5
crore of liabilities, and has 100 crore units outstanding, the NAV is

(1,000 - 5) / 100 = **INR 9.95 per unit**

**Important misconceptions:**

* A "low NAV" fund is **not** cheaper than a "high NAV" fund. Two funds with
  identical portfolios but different launch dates will have different NAVs.
  Future return depends on the underlying portfolio, not the absolute NAV
* NAV is updated **once per day** after AMCs report holdings to the RTA. AMFI
  rules require publication by 11:00 PM on every business day
* SIP units are allotted at the NAV of the day funds are realised by the AMC,
  not the date you set up the SIP

**For ETFs**, the situation is different. ETFs trade on the exchange like
stocks during market hours and have an intraday market price separate from
their indicative NAV. Authorised participants arbitrage gaps between the two,
keeping them close.

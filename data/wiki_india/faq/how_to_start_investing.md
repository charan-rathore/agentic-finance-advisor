A clean four-step starter framework for an Indian salaried investor:

**Step 1. Build an emergency fund.** 3 to 6 months of essential expenses,
parked in a liquid mutual fund or sweep-in FD. This comes before any equity SIP.

**Step 2. Get core insurance.** Term life if you have dependents, plus a family
health insurance policy. Without these, one bad year derails everything else.

**Step 3. Open a Direct plan mutual fund account.** Free options include MF
Central (run by RTAs), Coin (Zerodha), Groww, Kuvera, or the AMC websites
themselves. You do **not** need a Demat account for mutual fund SIPs.

**Step 4. Start one or two SIPs.**

* **Index fund** tracking Nifty 50 or Nifty 500 for long-term wealth (e.g.
  HDFC Index Nifty 50 Direct Growth, UTI Nifty 50 Direct Growth)
* **ELSS** if you file under the old tax regime and want Section 80C benefit
  (e.g. Mirae Asset ELSS Tax Saver, Axis ELSS Tax Saver, both Direct Growth)

Two funds is plenty. Adding a third or fourth fund typically replicates what
the first two already cover.

**Common mistakes to avoid:**

* Starting with a small-cap or sectoral fund. The volatility scares first-time
  investors into stopping the SIP at exactly the wrong moment
* Choosing Regular plans over Direct plans. The 0.50 to 1.00 percent fee gap
  compounds to lakhs of rupees over 20 years
* Treating SIP as savings. Equity SIPs need 5 plus years to deliver their edge

*Educational only. Tailor to your specific goals, horizon, and tax situation.*

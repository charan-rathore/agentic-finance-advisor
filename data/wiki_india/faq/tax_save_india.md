Tax saving in India depends entirely on **which regime you file under**.

**New tax regime (default since FY 2024):**

* No 80C, 80D, or HRA deductions
* Lower base slab rates plus a higher standard deduction (INR 75,000)
* Best for salaried investors with no home loan, modest insurance, and few
  deductions

**Old tax regime:**

* Section 80C up to INR 1.5 lakh per year. Eligible: ELSS, PPF, EPF, life
  insurance premium, home loan principal, NSC, tuition fees, NPS up to limit
* Section 80CCD(1B) additional INR 50,000 for NPS Tier 1 (over and above 80C)
* Section 80D up to INR 25,000 self plus INR 25,000 to 50,000 for parents
* HRA exemption if you pay rent and your salary structure includes HRA
* Best for investors who can fully utilise these deductions

**Quick rule:** If you can credibly use 80C plus 80D plus HRA to deduct INR 3
lakh or more from taxable income, the old regime usually wins. Below that, the
new regime is simpler and often better.

**The 80C product priority for an old-regime salaried investor:**

1. **EPF** (employer-provided, automatic. Don't ignore the deduction)
2. **Home loan principal** if applicable
3. **Term life insurance premium** if applicable
4. **PPF** for the safe sleeve, INR 50,000 to 70,000 per year
5. **ELSS** for the equity sleeve, the rest of the 80C limit

Plus **NPS Tier 1** for the additional INR 50,000 under 80CCD(1B), if you have
budget after maxing out 80C.

*Educational only. Run the actual numbers in both regimes for your case.*

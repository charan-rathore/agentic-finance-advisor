# Indian Fiscal Calendar and Key Dates

A retail investor in India lives on three overlapping calendars: the financial
year (FY), the income tax compliance calendar, and the Reserve Bank monetary
policy calendar. Knowing the dates avoids missed deadlines that cost real money.

## The financial year

* Indian FY runs from **1 April to 31 March**
* "FY 2025-26" or "FY26" both refer to 1 April 2025 to 31 March 2026
* The Union Budget is typically presented to Parliament on **1 February**, and
  takes effect from 1 April of the same year unless specified otherwise

## Personal tax compliance dates

| Event | Date |
|---|---|
| 80C investment deadline (old regime) | 31 March of the relevant FY |
| TDS certificates (Form 16) issued by employer | By 15 June |
| Advance tax instalments | 15 June, 15 September, 15 December, 15 March |
| ITR filing deadline (no audit, individuals) | 31 July |
| ITR filing deadline (with audit) | 31 October |
| Belated or revised return | 31 December |
| Linking of PAN with Aadhaar | Periodically extended; check the latest CBDT circular |

## Why January through March is the busiest tax window

Most salaried individuals lock down 80C, 80D, NPS, and HRA documentation in this
quarter because:

* Employer asks for proofs to finalise TDS for the year
* ELSS, PPF, and NPS contributions made by 31 March count toward that FY
* Mistakes in this window mean higher TDS and a bigger ITR filing burden later

## RBI monetary policy calendar

The MPC meets **six times** a year, typically in February, April, June, August,
October, and December. Each meeting publishes a written resolution and is
followed by a press conference. Markets price in the resolution within minutes.

## Market holidays and settlement

* Indian equity markets trade Monday to Friday, with no trading on declared
  holidays. The exchange calendar is published by NSE and BSE in late December
  for the following calendar year
* Equity settlement is **T+1** since January 2023 (Indian markets are among the
  fastest globally)
* Mutual fund cut-off is **3:00 PM** on a business day for same-day NAV
  applicability for liquid funds, equity funds use NAV of the day funds are
  realised by the AMC

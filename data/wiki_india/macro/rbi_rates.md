# RBI Policy Rates: What Each One Does

The Reserve Bank of India sets a small number of policy rates that cascade into
deposit rates, loan EMIs, and bond yields across the economy. Understanding
each one helps you read RBI Monetary Policy Committee (MPC) statements without
relying on second-hand commentary.

## The four headline rates

| Rate | What it is | Why it matters to you |
|---|---|---|
| **Repo rate** | Rate at which RBI lends overnight to banks against government securities | Floor for home loan, personal loan, and FD rates |
| **Reverse repo (now SDF)** | Rate at which RBI absorbs excess liquidity from banks | Effective floor on call money rates |
| **CRR (Cash Reserve Ratio)** | Percentage of bank deposits held as cash reserve with RBI, no interest | Higher CRR squeezes liquidity, raises lending rates |
| **SLR (Statutory Liquidity Ratio)** | Percentage of deposits banks must hold in approved securities | Affects banking system bond demand |

Approximate values for FY 2025-26 (verify from the latest RBI MPC statement):

* Repo rate: around 5.25 to 6.50 percent (cycle dependent)
* SDF (Standing Deposit Facility): repo minus 25 basis points
* MSF (Marginal Standing Facility): repo plus 25 basis points
* CRR: typically 4.0 to 4.5 percent
* SLR: typically 18 percent

## How a repo rate change reaches you

* RBI raises repo by 25 basis points
* Banks raise MCLR or repo-linked external benchmark rates within a quarter
* Floating-rate home loans reset within 3 to 6 months
* Bank deposit rates typically rise with a 1 to 2 month lag
* New government bond yields rise quickly, existing bond prices fall

The reverse holds when RBI cuts rates.

## RBI's mandate

The MPC has a flexible inflation targeting framework. The CPI inflation target is
**4 percent plus or minus 2 percent**, set jointly by RBI and the Central
Government for a rolling 5-year period. The MPC meets six times a year.

## Why this affects your investments

* Equity markets, particularly Bank Nifty, react to rate moves
* Debt funds with longer duration see capital gains when rates fall, capital losses when rates rise
* PPF rate is reset quarterly, loosely linked to government securities yields
* New FD investors should compare offered rates against the prevailing repo rate and term structure

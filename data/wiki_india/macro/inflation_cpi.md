# CPI Inflation in India: How to Read It

The Consumer Price Index (CPI) is the headline inflation measure that drives RBI
monetary policy and is the most relevant inflation number for retail investors.
It is published monthly by the Ministry of Statistics and Programme Implementation
(MOSPI), usually 12 days after the reference month.

## Headline composition (CPI Combined, base year 2012)

| Group | Approx weight |
|---|---|
| Food and beverages | 45.9 percent |
| Pan, tobacco, intoxicants | 2.4 percent |
| Clothing and footwear | 6.5 percent |
| Housing | 10.1 percent |
| Fuel and light | 6.8 percent |
| Miscellaneous | 28.3 percent |

Food has by far the highest weight, which is why monsoon, crop yields, and
mandi prices feed quickly into headline inflation.

## Headline vs core inflation

* **Headline CPI** is what MOSPI publishes
* **Core CPI** strips out food and fuel, the two most volatile groups
* The MPC watches both, but anchors longer-term policy decisions to core CPI
  trends because they reflect demand-side pressure rather than supply shocks

## Why this number matters to investors

* RBI's mandate is to keep CPI between 2 and 6 percent. Persistent breaches
  trigger rate hikes
* Real return on a fixed deposit is the FD rate minus CPI. A 7 percent FD with
  6 percent CPI gives a real return of about 1 percent
* Equity returns over very long periods have outpaced CPI by 5 to 7 percentage
  points historically. Below CPI, you are losing purchasing power

## Recent indicative range (FY 2025-26)

CPI inflation has typically run in the 3 to 6 percent band over the last 18
months, with food spikes in select months. For up-to-date numbers, check the
MOSPI release calendar.

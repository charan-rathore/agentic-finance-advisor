# SIP vs Lumpsum: Which One When

Both SIP and lumpsum are valid ways to invest in mutual funds. The right answer
depends on the **source of your money** and your **emotional capacity to handle
short-term volatility**.

## Definitions

* **SIP (Systematic Investment Plan).** A fixed amount, typically monthly, invested
  on a recurring schedule
* **Lumpsum.** A one-time investment of a larger amount, all on the same day

## Mathematical expectation

Over very long horizons, lumpsum has a slight statistical edge **if** the lumpsum
is invested at a non-elevated market valuation. The reason is simple: more of
your money is invested earlier and so compounds longer. Backtests on the Nifty 50
TRI over 20-year windows typically show lumpsum beating SIP by 1 to 2 percentage
points of CAGR on average.

The catch is the **dispersion**. Lumpsum results vary much more than SIP because
the entry day matters disproportionately. A 30 percent drawdown in the first year
of a lumpsum investment is psychologically very different from the same
drawdown after a SIP has been running for two years.

## Practical rules of thumb

* You earn a salary and invest each month. **Use a SIP.** This matches the
  rhythm of your income, removes timing decisions, and builds the discipline
  habit
* You received a bonus, an inheritance, or sale proceeds (lumpsum amount). **Use
  a STP (Systematic Transfer Plan)** from a liquid fund into your equity fund,
  spread over 6 to 12 months. This is a "lumpsum done as SIP", limiting timing risk
* You are absolutely sure markets are at fair value, you have a 7 plus year horizon,
  and you can stomach short-term losses without panic. **Lumpsum may be appropriate**

## STP: the middle path

A Systematic Transfer Plan tells the AMC to move a fixed amount from a debt fund
(usually liquid) to an equity fund every week, fortnight, or month. STPs typically
run for 6 to 24 months. The unspent balance earns liquid-fund returns until it is
transferred.

This is the recommended approach for any one-time amount above INR 1 lakh.

## Common mistake

Lumpsum into a single small-cap or sector fund based on a hot tip. The variance is
so high that even sophisticated investors avoid this. If you must take a sectoral
bet, size it as 5 to 10 percent of your overall portfolio.

# Emergency Fund: The First Thing to Build, Before Any SIP

An emergency fund is the cash reserve that lets you survive a financial shock
(job loss, medical emergency, car repair) without breaking long-term investments
or running up credit card debt. It is the foundation of every personal finance
plan and should usually be built before starting an equity SIP.

## How much you need

The standard rule is **3 to 6 months of essential monthly expenses**, where
"essential" means rent, food, utilities, EMIs, insurance premiums, school fees,
and minimum medication costs. Add 1 to 2 months if you are self-employed, the
sole earner in your household, or in a volatile industry.

## Where to keep it

The job of an emergency fund is to be **available within 24 to 48 hours**, not
to maximise return. Acceptable holding places are:

| Vehicle | Liquidity | Indicative return | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Savings bank account | Instant | 2.5 to 4 percent | Up to INR 5 lakh per bank covered by DICGC |
| Sweep-in FD | Instant on withdrawal | 4 to 6 percent | Auto-converts FD to savings on demand |
| Liquid mutual fund | T+1 redemption (instant for amounts up to INR 50,000) | 6 to 7 percent | RBI permits AMCs to offer instant redemption up to INR 50,000 |
| Short-duration fund | T+1 to T+2 | 6 to 8 percent | Mark-to-market risk, mild |

A practical mix is: 1 month of expenses in your savings account, the rest in a
liquid fund at the same AMC where you hold your equity SIPs.

## What an emergency fund is not

* It is not your tax-saving 80C investment. Do not use ELSS or PPF as the
  emergency fund. They have lock-ins
* It is not your wealth-building portfolio. Equity exposure here defeats the
  purpose, because equity drawdowns coincide with the recessions that trigger
  job losses
* It is not your goal-based saving for a 5-year house down payment

## How to build it without overwhelming yourself

* Set a SIP into a liquid mutual fund at 10 to 20 percent of monthly income
* Continue until the fund holds 3 to 6 months of expenses
* Rebalance once a year if your monthly expenses have grown materially
* After it is fully funded, redirect that SIP into your equity portfolio

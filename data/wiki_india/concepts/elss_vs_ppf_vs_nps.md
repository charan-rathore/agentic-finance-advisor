# ELSS vs PPF vs NPS: Which 80C Instrument Suits You

For investors filing under the **old tax regime**, Section 80C of the Income Tax
Act allows up to INR 1,50,000 of investment per year as a deduction. Three
products dominate this bucket: ELSS, PPF, and NPS Tier 1.

## Side-by-side comparison

| Attribute | ELSS | PPF | NPS Tier 1 |
|---|---|---|---|
| Asset class | Equity | Sovereign | Mix of equity and debt |
| Lock-in | 3 years per SIP instalment | 15 years (loan/partial after 7) | Until age 60 |
| Indicative return | 10 to 14 percent CAGR (historical) | 7.1 percent (Q1 FY26) | 8 to 10 percent (mix dependent) |
| Risk | Equity (high) | Sovereign (zero) | Market (medium) |
| Section 80C limit | INR 1.5 lakh | INR 1.5 lakh | INR 1.5 lakh + extra INR 50,000 under 80CCD(1B) |
| Maturity tax | LTCG 12.5 percent above INR 1.25 lakh | Tax-free (EEE) | 60 percent corpus tax-free at 60, 40 percent must buy annuity (taxable) |
| Liquidity | After lock-in | Partial after year 7, loan after year 3 | Very low until age 60 |
| Best for | Long-horizon equity comfort + tax saving | Safety-first, retirement, child education | Retirement supplement, additional 80CCD(1B) |

## How to choose

* If your time horizon is 15 plus years and you want zero risk, **PPF** is the
  default safety sleeve
* If your horizon is 5 plus years and you are comfortable with equity volatility,
  **ELSS** has historically delivered the highest post-tax return per year of
  lock-in
* If you specifically want a retirement vehicle with the additional INR 50,000
  deduction under 80CCD(1B), **NPS Tier 1** complements ELSS and PPF

## A reasonable default split for a 30-percent-bracket salaried investor

* PPF: INR 50,000 to 70,000 per year (safety, EEE)
* ELSS: INR 80,000 to 100,000 per year (equity growth, 3-year lock-in)
* NPS Tier 1: INR 50,000 per year for the extra 80CCD(1B) deduction (retirement)

This is illustrative, not advice. The exact split depends on your existing EPF,
home loan principal, life insurance premium, and tax bracket.

## Important: the new tax regime

The new tax regime (default since FY 2024) **does not allow 80C, 80D, or 80CCD**
deductions. Compare the two regimes before you invest. For salaried investors with
no home loan and modest insurance premiums, the new regime is often simpler and
better, in which case ELSS still works as an investment but loses its tax edge.

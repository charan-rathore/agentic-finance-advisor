# Market Cap Categories: Large, Mid, Small (the SEBI definitions)

SEBI's mutual fund categorisation circular (October 2017, updated periodically)
defines market cap buckets unambiguously. AMFI publishes the universe every six
months. These definitions matter because they decide what each fund category
can hold.

## The official ranking

* **Large cap.** Companies ranked **1 to 100** by full market capitalisation
* **Mid cap.** Companies ranked **101 to 250**
* **Small cap.** Companies ranked **251 and below**

The list is recomputed every six months by AMFI based on the average full
market cap of every listed Indian equity, then published. As of FY 2025-26,
the threshold for "large cap" is approximately INR 70,000 crore market cap.
Anything below the rank-100 line (about INR 36,000 crore) is mid cap, and
anything below the rank-250 line (about INR 11,000 crore) is small cap.

## How fund categories map

| Fund category | What it must hold |
|---|---|
| Large Cap | At least 80 percent in large caps |
| Large and Mid Cap | At least 35 percent in large + 35 percent in mid |
| Mid Cap | At least 65 percent in mid caps |
| Small Cap | At least 65 percent in small caps |
| Multi Cap | At least 25 percent each in large, mid, and small |
| Flexi Cap | At least 65 percent in equity, no cap-bucket floor (manager's discretion) |
| Focused | Maximum 30 stocks, any cap |
| ELSS | At least 80 percent in equity (any cap), 3-year lock-in |

## Risk-return character of each bucket

| Bucket | Volatility | Liquidity | Long-run return potential |
|---|---|---|---|
| Large cap | Lower | Very high | 10 to 13 percent CAGR (historical, indicative) |
| Mid cap | Higher | Moderate | 12 to 16 percent CAGR (historical, indicative) |
| Small cap | Highest | Lower | 13 to 18 percent CAGR (historical, indicative) but with 50-plus percent drawdowns possible |

These ranges are descriptive, not predictive. Past performance does not guarantee
future returns.

## A practical default for first-time investors

* 60 to 80 percent of equity in **large cap or flexi cap**
* 0 to 20 percent in **mid cap** if your horizon is 10 plus years
* 0 to 15 percent in **small cap**, only if you can stomach drawdowns

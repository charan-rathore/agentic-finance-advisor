# Systematic Investment Plan (SIP)

A Systematic Investment Plan is an instruction you give a mutual fund to debit a
fixed amount from your bank account every month and buy units of the fund at the
prevailing NAV. SIPs are the dominant way Indian retail investors put money into
mutual funds.

## Why SIPs work

1. **Rupee-cost averaging.** When NAV is low, your fixed rupee amount buys more
   units. When NAV is high, it buys fewer. Over a long horizon this smooths the
   average purchase price.
2. **Compounding.** Returns earn returns. INR 5,000 invested every month at a
   12 percent annual return compounds to roughly INR 11.6 lakh in 10 years and
   INR 49.9 lakh in 20 years.
3. **Behavioural defence.** A standing instruction removes the daily decision
   of whether to invest, which is where most retail investors lose to their
   own emotions.

## How a SIP gets executed

* You set up a SIP through any AMFI-registered platform (Coin, Groww, Kuvera,
  MF Central, AMC website)
* On each SIP date, the fund's RTA (CAMS or KFin Technologies) collects the
  amount via NACH or UPI Autopay
* Units are allotted at the NAV of the day funds are realised
* Annual statements are generated by the RTA

## How long a SIP should run

Equity SIPs need 5 plus years to express their statistical edge. Three years is
the absolute minimum for ELSS because of the lock-in. Anything shorter is
saving, not investing.

## Common mistakes

* Stopping a SIP during a market crash. Drawdowns are when SIPs accumulate the
  most units per rupee
* Spreading a small SIP across 6 to 8 funds. Two well-chosen funds is plenty
* Choosing Regular plans over Direct plans. The 0.50 to 1.00 percent fee gap
  compounds significantly

## Relevant SEBI and AMFI rules

* No exit load on SIPs from index funds and most equity funds beyond the holding
  threshold (typically 365 days)
* Mutual funds must publish daily NAV by 11:00 PM on every business day (AMFI rule)
* Riskometer disclosure is mandatory and updated monthly

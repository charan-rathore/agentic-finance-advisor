# Capital Gains Tax in India: LTCG and STCG After Budget 2024

The Union Budget 2024 (presented 23 July 2024) materially changed the capital
gains tax framework. Here is what applies for FY 2024-25 and onwards.

## Equity (stocks and equity-oriented mutual funds with at least 65 percent equity)

| Holding period | Tax category | Rate |
|---|---|---|
| Less than 12 months | Short-Term Capital Gain (STCG) | **20 percent** flat (was 15 percent before Budget 2024) |
| 12 months or more | Long-Term Capital Gain (LTCG) | **12.5 percent** above INR 1,25,000 per FY (was 10 percent above INR 1,00,000) |

The annual LTCG exemption rose from INR 1 lakh to **INR 1.25 lakh per FY** as
part of the same Budget. The LTCG rate also rose from 10 to 12.5 percent.
On balance, most retail equity investors pay slightly more tax than before.

### Worked example

* Bought INR 5,00,000 of a Nifty 50 index fund in January 2024
* Sold for INR 6,50,000 in February 2026
* Holding period exceeds 12 months. LTCG applies
* Total gain: INR 1,50,000
* Exempt portion: INR 1,25,000
* Taxable: INR 25,000
* Tax at 12.5 percent: **INR 3,125**

Note: this exemption resets every financial year. Many investors deliberately
"book and rebuy" up to INR 1.25 lakh of long-term gains every March to refresh
their cost basis tax-free. This is called LTCG harvesting and is fully legal.

## Debt mutual funds (post Finance Act 2023)

All debt mutual fund gains, regardless of holding period, are taxed at your
**slab rate** if you bought units on or after 1 April 2023. There is no LTCG
benefit and no indexation.

## Listed bonds

| Holding period | Rate |
|---|---|
| Less than 12 months | Slab rate (STCG) |
| 12 months or more | 12.5 percent without indexation (LTCG) |

## Gold (post Budget 2024)

| Vehicle | Holding | Rate |
|---|---|---|
| Physical gold, gold ETF, gold MF | Less than 24 months | Slab rate |
| Physical gold, gold ETF, gold MF | 24 months or more | 12.5 percent (no indexation) |
| Sovereign Gold Bond, held to 8-year maturity | Maturity | **Tax-free** |

SGBs continue to be the most tax-efficient long-horizon gold holding.

## Real estate

| Holding period | Rate |
|---|---|
| Less than 24 months | Slab rate |
| 24 months or more | **12.5 percent without indexation** OR 20 percent with indexation, taxpayer's choice for property bought before 23 July 2024 |

Budget 2024 removed indexation benefit on property sales after 23 July 2024.
Sellers of older property may still elect the 20 percent with indexation route.

## Important reminders

* LTCG on equity above the INR 1.25 lakh exemption is taxed even if your total
  income is below the basic exemption limit
* You must self-report capital gains in ITR-2 or ITR-3. Resident Indian mutual
  fund redemptions do not have TDS deducted by the AMC
* Carry-forward of capital losses is permitted for up to 8 assessment years
